
### Problem 2: Negative Form Construction
#### Rules:
1. **Marker Placement:**
   - Insert the marker `-(k)a’-` after the first syllable if it is either closed (ends in a consonant) or long (contains a long vowel).
   - Otherwise, insert the marker after the second syllable.

2. **Vowel Length:**
   - If the marker is inserted after a long vowel, the vowel loses its length.

3. **Marker Form:**
   - After an open syllable, the marker retains its original form `-ka’-`.
   - After a closed syllable, it changes to `-a’-`.

4. **Special Case for Mono- and Disyllabic Words:**
   - The marker appears as `-(k)a:ʔa’`, where (k) depends on Rule 3.

#### Examples:
1. **Word Analysis:**
   - `bakwanyiń` (‘my wrist’) → `kas bakwanyiń` (‘not my wrist’).
   - The marker `-ka’-` is inserted after the second syllable, indicating the first syllable is open.

2. **Base Forms and Negative Forms:**
   | Base Form          | Negative Form          |
   |--------------------|------------------------|
   | `as` (to sit)      | `kas asa:sa’`          |
   | `enferme:ra`       | `kas ena’ferme:ra`     |
   | `ji!a:pa`          | `kas ji!aka’pa`        |
   | `de`               | `kas deka:ka’`         |
   | `rulrul`           | `kas rula’rul`         |
   | `tipoysu:da`       | `kas tipoya’su:da`     |
   | `wurul`            | `kas wurula:la’`       |
   | `dewajna`          | `kas dewaja’na`        |
   | `de:wajna`         | `kas deka’wajna`       |

### Problem 3: Georgian Verb Structure
#### Analysis:
1. **Components Identification:**
   - Suffixes: `-e`, `-ob`, `-eb`.
   - Prefixes: `v-`, `i-`.

2. **Person/Number Markers:**
   - Prefix and suffix combinations mark person and number:
     - `v+0`, `0+0`, `v+t`, `0+t`.

3. **Verb Forms and Meanings:**
   - `vtkvi` — I said.
   - `nadirob` — you hunt.
   - `kenit` — you (pl.) did.
   - `visadileb` — I will dine.
   - `inadiret` — you (pl.) hunted.
   - `vinadire` — I hunted.
   - `itavmGdomareve` — you chaired.
   - `ambob` — you say.
   - `vsadilobt` — we dine.
   - `vitavmGdomareve` — I chaired.
   - `tkvi` — you said.
   - `izamt` — you (pl.) will do.
   - `vigoreb` — I will roll.
   - `vivlit` — we will walk.
   - `vkeni` — I did.

4. **Tense Markers:**
   - Present: `-ob`.
   - Future: `i-`.

5. **Strong vs. Weak Verbs:**
   - Weak verbs: `i-` in the past, `-e` suffix in the past, `-eb` suffix in the future.
   - Strong verbs: No suffixes, different roots for tenses.

### Problem 4: Numerical System Analysis
#### Rules:
1. **Base 6 System:**
   - Numbers are expressed in base 6.
   - `36` (base) is marked by the word `nif`.

2. **Multiplication and Addition:**
   - `an` marks multiplication by 6.
   - `abo` marks addition.

#### a. Number Translations:
1. `mer an thef abo thonith` = 16 (6 × 2 + 4)
2. `nif thef abo mer abo ithin` = 81 (36 × 2 + 6 + 3)
3. `nif abo mer an thef abo sas` = 49 (36 + 12 + 1)
4. `nif abo tondor abo mer abo thonith` = 64 (36 + 18 + 6 + 4)
5. `nif thef abo tondor abo mer abo thonith` = 100 (72 + 18 + 6 + 4)
6. tondor abo mer abo sas = 18+6+1 = 25
7. mer abo ithin = 6+3 = 9
8. thonith = 4
9. sas = 1
10. nif = 6& = 36

#### b. Additional Examples:

1. `mer abo sas` = 7 (6 + 1)
2. `tondor abo mer abo sas` = 18+6*2+x = 30+x

#### c. Numerical Equivalents:
1. nif ithin abo ithin = 6^2 3+3 = 111
2. mer an thef abo meregh = 6*2+5 = 17

### Problem 5: Turkish and Tatar Sound Correspondences
#### Observations:
1. **Vowel Correspondences:**
   - `a ~ a`, `i` (1st syllable) ~ `i`, `e` (non-1st syllable) ~ `ä`.
   - `o ~ u`, `ö ~ ü`.
   - `ı` (non-1st syllable) ~ `ı`.

2. **Consonant Correspondences:**
   - `m` (medial) ~ `m`, `m` (initial) ~ `m` if `n` follows.
   - `b ~ b` (initial), `d ~ d`, `t ~ t`.
   - `n ~ n`, `l ~ l`, `r ~ r`, `s ~ s`, `z ~ z`.

3. **Special Consonant Patterns:**
   - `c ~ ç` before Tatar `i`.
   - `y ~ y` elsewhere.
   - `) ~ g`, `g` (initial) ~ word-initially.
   - `k ~ k` elsewhere.
