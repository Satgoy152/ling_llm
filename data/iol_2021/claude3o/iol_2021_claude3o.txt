Problem 1:

(a)  
moeto waroewo = 160
ijema joka rati = 59 (same as ije mepija)
moeto wido ma benomi ka wi = 141

(b) 
1 = wi
19 = benomima mepija OR ije ka wi
26 = widoma benomi 
104 = moeto idibi ma idibi
292 = moeto idibi ma enama rati beo daemita wija  

The Ekari number system uses a base of 20. The numbers 1-5 are: wi, wija, waroewo, idibi, idi. 6-9 add -ma to 1-4. 10 = ije. 11-19 use ije plus 1-9. 
20 = benomi, 40 = daemita wija, 60 = daemita waroewo, etc.  
Multiples of 20 can also be formed by moeto (= "man") plus a number 1-19.  
100 = moeto idi.

In Zuni:
Compounds: AB = "A of B", e.g. ma:chikwa "loaf of bread" = ma:- "bread" + chikwa "egg" 
Plurals: ABA = "Bs of A", e.g. mu:chikwa "beads" = mu:we "bead" + chikwa "egg"
Suffixes: 
-we = singulative 
-le = collective (e.g. a pile or field of something)
-nne = plural (of animates)
-yanne = leaf

Observations on Kilivila:
- Verb-initial word order
- Subject/object markers on verbs:
   le-: 3sg subject, le-...-si: 3pl subject
   b-: future + 3sg subj, b-...-si: fut + 3pl subj 
- Demonstratives: mtona "this", minana "this", makena "that", magudiwena "that"
   Plural: mtosina, minasina, makesina, magudisina  
- Question words: amakena "which", amminana "which", navila/kwevila "how many"
- Numbers: natala "one", nayu "two", ketala "one long object", kweyu "two long objects", tevila "how many people" 
- Adjectives after nouns, e.g. gwadi magudiwena gudimanabweta "that beautiful child"

The derivation of Agbirigba words involves infixing -tV- after each vowel, where V is a high vowel agreeing with the preceding vowel in backness and rounding. Sequences of vowel + nasal are treated as units. Tones are copied along with the vowels.

Key observations:
- Verbs show subject agreement: 
  a- "I", tsi- "you_{sg}", ni- "he/they", tsi-...-naha "we", tsi-...-naha "you_{pl}"
- Verbs also mark tense/aspect:
  -wak "future/potential", unmarked for non-future. 
- Object pronouns are infixed in the verb:
  -ka- "me/us", -ha- "you_{pl}", -kaha- "you_{pl} object + I/we subject"
- Verb roots: 
  -pery "wait for", -ksi "leave", -puru "sleep", -mywak "hunt", -moro/-myboro "bathe", -zomo "arrive", -boro "bite", -knara "fall", -beze "kill", -wy "cut up", -rapyryk "hug"
