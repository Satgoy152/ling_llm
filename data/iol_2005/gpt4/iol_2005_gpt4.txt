### Problem 5

#### Assignment 1: Determine which accent paradigms originally belonged together.

Paradigms 1 and 2 belonged together, as did paradigms 3 and 4.

#### Assignment 2: Determine what the initial accent paradigms looked like.

| **Paradigm** | **1 & 2**  | **3 & 4**  |
|--------------|------------|------------|
| **Nom. Sg.** | lìepà       | galvà       |
| **Gen. Sg.** | lìepòs     | galvòs     |
| **Nom. Pl.** | lìepòs     | galvòs     |
| **Acc. Pl.** | lìepàs     | galvàs     |

#### Assignment 3: Formulate Saussure’s Law.

Saussure's Law states that if a word's syllable of the first type was accented and the ending was of the second type, the accent moved to the ending. This created a new accent paradigm with the accent on the ending in the Genitive Singular and Nominative Plural, and on the root in the Accusative Plural. This explains the division into four paradigms from the original two.
