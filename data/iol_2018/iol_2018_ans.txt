Problem 4 Solutions

(a) The word that the linguist initially thought was missing is “sugar canes,” which are represented by the word arah. 
The three examples are:

	1.	6 sugar canes — biogu nybat
	2.	10 sugar canes — biogu nybat atuh araruh
	3.	23 sugar canes — biogu atugu biogu biaruh atuh araruh

    (6, 9, 11): 4 =nybat,8=biogu nybat,k×4(3≤k)=k-t/gunybat

In these examples, the term “sugar canes” is implied by context and number markers rather than being explicitly stated.

(b) Translations into English:

	12.	biabys atub bøbys — 3 betel nuts
	13.	atub juhurub — 1 egg
	14.	nybat atuh araruh — 5 sugar canes
	15.	biagas atugas biagas baigas — 5 sheets of sago bark
	16.	wiwis baigas — 12 sheets of sago bark

(c) Translations into Mountain Arapesh:
