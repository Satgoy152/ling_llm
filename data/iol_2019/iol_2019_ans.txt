Problem 1 Solutions

(a) Translations into English:
12. Om benu aneen. — She took the bread and ate it.
13. Munuuna wunuub. — She comes and we go away.
14. Wingkiiwa wengamburuun. — They sing and she hears.
15. Anon ye weng wengamberenib yeedaraniib. — They will hear the dog and will get up.
16. Oon wedmaniina karub wedmaneeb. — I will see the fish and you(sg) will see the man.
17. Ok wedmeena aniin. — He sees the water and I drink it.

(b) Translations into Yonggom:
18. We will go away and they will come. — Wananuuwa mananiib.
19. I take the pig and he takes the fish. — Awon biina oon been.
20. He saw the water and the house and heard the dog. — Oka ambiwa wedmene anon ye weng wengamboroon.
21. You(sg) eat the bread and sing. — Om aneneb wingkeeb.

Problem 5 Solutions

(a) Translations into English:
16. bvúsɔ̀w nǔ fɔ̀ shě ntfǔː. — Bvusɔw is the day after tomorrow.
17. me nú ŋgéː nyɛ́n kèŋkfǔ lé ɛ̀bɛ̀n. — Today, I will see the yam.
18. wvù tɔ̀ nɔ̂ː yɛ᷅n bèsèn ě bvúmbòn. — On Bvumbon, he saw us.
19. bèsèn nǔ bóy tɔ́ fɔ̀wǎy bvúzhı ᷇ː de᷆n. — On Bvuzhiden, we will come to the market.

(b) Order of the Nooni days:

	•	Bvutfu, Bvuŋka, Bvuzhi, Bvukema, Bvuŋkaden, Bvuzhiden, Bvusɔw, Bvumbon.

(c) Translations into Nooni:
20. On Bvusɔw, I helped the man. — me ncí nɔ̂ː nfı ᷅ dìɛ̀msɛ̌n bvúsɔ̀w.
21. The thief stole the yam just now. — cɔ̌ŋ nɔ̂ː cɔ᷅ŋ kèŋkfǔ.
22. On Bvuŋkaden, I will hear the car. — me nú nyúː nyów mutù bvûŋka᷆ːde᷆n.
23. Today, the woman will kill the man. — kwɔ᷆ːn nǔ géː yó dìɛ̀msɛ̌n ɛ̀bɛ̀n.
24. Today, the man saw you(sg). — dìɛ̀msɛ̌n bɛ̂ː yɛ᷅n wɔ̀ lě ɛ̀bɛ̀n.
