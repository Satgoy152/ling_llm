(a) Rules of Dróttkvætt Meter

Dróttkvætt is a complex Old Norse poetic meter characterized by strict rules:

	1.	Syllable Count:
	•	Each line contains exactly six syllables.
	2.	Alliteration:
	•	First Line: Contains two words that begin with the same consonant or any vowel.
	•	Second Line: The first stressed syllable must begin with the same sound as the alliterative sound in the first line.
	3.	Internal Rhymes (Hendingar):
	•	Odd Lines (Skothendingar): Feature a half-rhyme where the consonants are the same but vowels differ between two stressed syllables (usually the second and fifth syllables).
	•	Even Lines (Aðalhendingar): Feature a full rhyme where both consonants and vowels are the same between two stressed syllables (usually the second and fifth syllables).
	4.	Stress Pattern:
	•	Each line has three stressed syllables.
	5.	No Unstressed Line Endings:
	•	Lines should not end with an unstressed syllable.

Example Analysis:

	•	III:3–4
	•	Alliteration: rausnarsamr, rimmu, ríðviggs (all starting with r).
	•	Internal Rhyme:
	•	Line 3 (Odd): Skothending between rausnarsamr and rimmu (half-rhyme).
	•	Line 4 (Even): Aðalhending between ríðviggs and skíðum (full rhyme).
