## Problem 2: Apurinã

**(a)**

17. uwa xinhikaru sytu  - **The woman remembered my eye.**
18. txipary nhinhikary - **I remembered your banana.**
19. kyky aikute pysykary ywamukary - **The man brought his eye to me.**

**(b)**  uwa umynary kanawa ximakymukary 

This sentence is incorrect because the possessor of 'canoe' (kanawa) should be marked with a possessive prefix agreeing with the subject of the verb 'bring' (umynary). 

**Corrected Sentence:** uwa umynary **ysy**kanawa ximakymukary
**English Translation:** He brought the woman's canoe to the fish.

**(c)**

21. You_{sg} found the house.
     * **Naikute paapukary.**
     * **Paapukary naikute.** 

22. I brought your_{sg} canoe to the girl.
     * **Nuta mupukary pisykaru hãtakurumukary.** 

23. He brought your_{sg} water to the me.
     * **Uwa umynary pĩpurããte nutamukary.** 

24. The man drank the girl’s blood.
     * **Kyky ãtary hãtakuru sytumukary.** 

25. I remembered the eye.
     * **Nuta inhikary ywa.** 

26. You_{sg} ate my banana.
     * **Pita apĩtxi nhitxiparyte.**

**Explanation:** 

Apurinã uses a system of prefixes and suffixes to indicate grammatical relations. 
* **Object prefixes** on verbs agree with the person and number of the object. 
* **Possessive prefixes** on nouns agree with the possessor. 
* Word order is somewhat flexible, allowing for variations in emphasis.

## Problem 5: Supyire

**(a)**

* kampwɔhii shuunni na kɛ  - **1007**  (1000 (kampwɔhii) + 7 (shuunni) )
* ŋkuu na baataanre - **120** (100 (ŋkuu) + 20 (baataanre))

**(b)** 

* 15 - **taanre na niŋkin** (10 (taanre) + 5 (niŋkin))
* 109 - **ŋkuu na kaŋkuro** (100 (ŋkuu) + 9 (kaŋkuro))
* 152 - **ŋkuu na taanre na niŋkin na baani** (100 (ŋkuu) + 10 (taanre) + 2 (baani))
* 403 - **kampwoo shicyɛɛre na beeshuunni** (400 (kampwoo shicyɛɛre) + 3 (beeshuunni))
* 1534 - **kampwɔhii sicyɛɛre na ŋkwuu taanre na beeshuunni na baashuunni** (1000 (kampwɔhii sicyɛɛre) + 500 (ŋkwuu taanre na beeshuunni) + 30 (beeshuunni na taanre) + 4 (baashuunni))

**Explanation:** 

Supyire has a base-5 numeral system with elements of a base-20 system. 

* **Basic Numbers:** The system uses basic number words for 1-5, 10, 20, and 1000.
* **Combining Numbers:** Larger numbers are formed by combining these basic units, often using "na" as a conjunction. 
* **Multiplicative Construction:**  Notice the use of multiplication for some numbers (e.g., 400 is "kampwoo shicyɛɛre," literally "four hundreds").

By understanding these patterns, we can express the given numbers in Supyire.
