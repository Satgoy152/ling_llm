Here is my solution to the computational linguistics problems:

Problem 1:

(a)

15. netkay' ɨnnetaɡ'a push 
16. kɨrɨy' ɨŋɡɨr'ɨ pull
17. p'uhruy' ɨmp'uhar'u make holes
18. her'oy ɨnder'o smooth out  

For verbs ending in -Vy', Form 2 starts with ɨn- or ɨŋ- and ends in -V, -Vʔ, or -VʔV. Consonant changes: p' -> mp', k -> ɡ, t -> d, ʦ -> ʦ, ʂ -> ʦ, r' -> r', h -> h. No change for n, m, ŋ, l', w, y.

(b)  

39. her'oŋ    I smoothed out (it)
40. ɨniʂpaʔ   I left                 
41. aplaʔ     it opened         
42. ʂur'uʔ    it chose (it)        
43. piiriŋ    I saw (it)        
44. ʂawʂaŋ   I plant (it)          
45. ak'aniʔ   it was trapped         
46. ap'ɨhnay' I leap             
47. ahɨk'aʔ   it is being sewn, woven
48. ɨniplaʔ   I was bathed           

For "progressive" forms like "I am being seen", add ɨn- or ɨm- and -ʔ. Other 1sg forms end in -ŋ or -y'. 3sg intransitive forms start with a- and end in -ʔ or -Vʔ.

Problem 2:

(a)

(b) 

20. This is incorrect because umynary "your_{sg} eye" requires a 1st person subject. The correct sentence is:

"I remembered the eye."

"You_{sg} ate my banana."

Apurinã has free word order. Possessed nouns agree with the possessor in person (-nu my, -py your_{sg}, -wa his/her). Object pronouns are nutV-, pitV-, ywV-, etc. Subject agreement is n(V)- "I", p(V)- "you_{sg}", zero "he/she".

Problem 3:

(a)   

18. You_{pl} shuddered.
19. Aw, you_{pl} cried.  
20. Oh, we spat on you_{pl} in vain!
21. We really left you_{pl} behind. 
22. Aw, you_{sg} laughed at him in vain.  

(b)

23. a-tamanab
24. ubate-dahetok  
25. uba-esanab
26. nake-otaɣab  
27. ebakam-keseh  

Coastal Marind verbs agree with both subject and object. Prefixes: a- "he", nV- "I", bV- "you_{pl}", zero "you_{sg}". Subject suffixes: -m "I", -b "you_{pl}", zero "he/you_{sg}". Object suffixes (after -n): -ab "us", -eb "him", -ib "me", -Vwn "you_{pl}" (V = last vowel of prefix). Other suffixes: -ewn "left behind vainly", -awn "left behind", -aɣah/-anah "really", -ah/-ab "exclamative".

Problem 4:

(b)

26. kakwēcimāyāhkwāwi
27. kimawineskomikwak

29. kiwāpamāwak
30. kinakinitināwāw

Plains Cree verbs mark both subject and object. ni-/-i- "I", ki- "you_{sg}/you_{pl}", zero "he/they". -ā- marks a 3sg object, -iko- a 1pl/2pl object, -i a 1sg/2sg object. Final -n marks a 1sg/2sg object, -nān a 1pl object, -wāw a 2pl object, -w a 3sg object, -wak/-k a 3pl object. ē-...-ak "as I...", ē-...-āyēk "as you_{pl}...", ...-ci "if 2sg...", ...-iko "if 1pl...", ...-āhkwāwi "if 3pl..."

(b)  kɛ na kaŋkuro 
     ŋkwɔ sicyɛɛre na baaricyɛɛre
     ŋkwɔ baataanre na beeshuunni na shuunni

The Supyire number system is partly decimal, partly vigesimal. 1-10: baani, shuunni, taanre, sicyɛɛre, kaŋkuro, baani...kɛ. Teens: kɛ na baani...kɛ na kɛ. Round 10's: beeshuunni, beetaanre, beesicyɛɛre...ŋkwuu. 100's: ŋkwɔ baani...ŋkwɔ kɛ. ŋkwuu = 20, kampwɔ = 400.
