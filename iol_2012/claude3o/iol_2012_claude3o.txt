Here are the solutions to the problems:

Problem 1:

(a) The animal regarded as "old women" in Dyirbal myths is the eel (waymin). The linguist likely thought balan waymin ("the mother-in-law") in sentence 3 was an error, expecting bayi waymin instead since waymin is an animate noun. But mythological associations can affect noun classes.

(b) English translations:
17. The health is making the father that is always searching tired.  
18. The lizard that is tired is searching for the stone that is always being pushed.
19. The wallaby is following the doctor that the quiet man is always blaming.

(c) Dyirbal translations:
20. bala yila wura baNgul duNangu buóan.
21. balan mugunanéa baNgun yaóaNgu banéalmuNagu munduman. 
22. bayi midin munduNgu yaóaNgu NaéilmuNagu muNgan.
23. bayi bayimbam baNgul yaóaNgu dibanbilmbalNaymuNagu guniñu.

Problem 3:

(a) Correspondences are correct.

(b) Basque translations:
