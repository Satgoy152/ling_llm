### Problem 1: Word Order and Transformations
#### Rules:
1. **Word Order:** OSV (Object, Subject, Verb) and NA (Noun, Adjective).
2. **Transformations:**
   - A → V (‘make A’): A-man.
   - V → A:
     - ‘that is always being Ved’ → `-n -ñu`
     - ‘that is always Ving Ns’ → `-l-muNa` / `-l-Nay-muNa`

3. **Articles:**
   - **O S:**
     - `balan` `baNgun` — women, dangerous animals, and objects.
     - `bayi` `baNgul` — men, animals.
     - `bala` `baNgu` — all other things.

4. **Subject and Attributes Endings:**
   - `-Ngu` if word ends in a vowel and has two syllables.
   - `-gu` if word ends in a vowel and has more than two syllables.
   - `-Du` if word ends in a consonant (D is a stop articulated in the same place in the mouth as the final sound of the word).

#### (a)
- The grasshopper is neither a woman nor a dangerous animal, but takes the same article, so it must be the “old woman” from the myth. The linguist thought that `baNgun bundiñéu` in example (14) was an error.

#### (b)
1. `balan ñalNga baNgul NumaNgu guniymuNagu bambunman.`  
   (The father that is always being searched for is healing the girl.)

2. `bala diban bilmbalmuNa baNgun biñéiriñéu guniñu.`  
   (The lizard is searching for the stone that is always being pushed.)

3. `bayi bargan baNgul yaóaNgu gubimbuluNunéanaymuNagu banéan.`  
   (The man that is always blaming doctors is following the wallaby.)

#### (c)
1. The little wallaby is looking at the dragonfly.  
   `bayi yiriñéila baNgul bargandu wuraNgu buóan.`

2. The aunt that is always being followed is bending the feather.  
   `bala yila baNgun mugunanéagu banéalmuNagu waóuman.`

3. The sleeping possum is ignoring the loud noise.  
   `bala muNga baNgul midindu éagundu Naéin.`

4. The caterpillar is searching for the man that is always carrying stones.  
   `bayi yaóa dibandimbanaymuNa baNgul bayimbambu guniñu.`

### Problem 2: Numeral System
#### Umbu-Ungu Numerals
1. telu
2. talu
3. yepoko

12. rurepo
16. malapu
20. supu
24. tokapu
28. alapu
32. polangipu

#### (a) Translations
1. `tokapu polangipu` = 24 + 32 = 56
2. `tokapu talu rureponga telu` = 24×2 + 12¬3 = 57
3. `tokapu yepoko malapunga talu` = 24×3 + 16¬2 = 86
4. `tokapu yepoko polangipunga telu` = 24×3 + 32¬1 = 101

#### (b) Translations
1. `malapunga telu` = 16¬1 = 13
2. `tokapu talu supunga talu` = 24×2 + 20¬2 = 66
3. `tokapu yepoko` = 24×3 = 72
4. `tokapu talu alapu` = 24×2 + 28 = 76
5. `tokapu yepoko tokapunga yepoko` = 24×3 + 24¬3 = 95

### Problem 4: Sentence Structure
#### Rules:
1. **Structure:** S `paa` V O [O′ ]
   - O `paa` V S [O′ ]
   - O′ `paa` V S O
2. **Articles:**
   - `a` if this is the first third person in the sentence.
   - `bona` otherwise.

#### (a) Translations
1. `Eam paa ani a overe.` — You (pl.) ate the coconut.
2. `Ean paa tasu a oraoraa bona kae.` — You (sg.) struck the sorcerer with the bag.
3. `Eove paa tara ameam.` — He saw you (pl.).

#### (b) Translations
1. `Enam paa hee vuan a taba’ani.` — We gave the food to you (sg.).
2. `Eove paa dao anaa bona beiko.` — He called me a child.
3. `Enaa paa asun e bona.` — I killed him with it.
4. `A oraoraa paa hee bona visoasi bona iana.` — The sorcerer gave the fish to the boy.

#### (c) Translations
1. They called the sorcerer a woman.  
   `A oraoraa paa dao ori bona moon.`

2. The boy killed the fish with the axe.  
   `A toraara paa asun a visoasi bona iana.`

### Problem 5: Morphological Changes
#### Morphological Rules
1. `-VCV` → `-VC`
2. `-VCu` → `-VC`
3. `-VCi` → `-V̈C`
4. `-VCa` → `-VaC`

#### (a) Body Parts:
1. `‘u‘u` — arm/hand
2. `isu` — nose
3. `kia` — neck
4. `leva` — hair
5. `mafa` — eye
6. `susu` — breast
7. `huga` — heart

#### (b) Additional Translations:
1. `tiro` — glass (stuff)
2. `poga` — hole
3. `huag lala` — patient
4. `ha. f puhra. ki` — volcanic rock
5. `maf pogi` or `maf pala` — blind

#### (c) Compound Words:
1. `round` — `ka. lka. lu`
2. `to cut copra` — `‘ol niu`
3. `curly hair` — `leav pirpiri`
4. `sticky` — `pulpulu`
5. `to flash` — `rima`
6. `rubbish` — `mofa`

#### (d) Special Forms:
1. Word: `fäega` (or `fäeaga`, `fäeagu`)
2. To exhaust: `a‘ofi` (or `a‘öfi`, `a‘öfö`, `a‘öfu`, `a‘öfü`, `a‘ofü`)
