### Problem 1: Phonological Rules
#### Rules:
1. **Apostrophe (`'`):**
   - Follows a vowel: indicates length.
   - Follows a consonant: read as [ə].

2. **Letter 'w':**
   - After a consonant: rounding of the lips.
   - Otherwise: [w].

3. **[ə] Pronunciation:**
   - Between any consonant and a following sonorant consonant ([l, m, n]).
   - Before a consonant cluster at the beginning of a word.

4. **Consonant Voicing:**
   - `p t j g gw q qw` are voiced ([b d j g gw G Gw]) at the beginning of a word or between vowels.
   - Voiceless ([p t c k kw x xw]) at the end of a word or next to another consonant.

### Problem 2: Old Norse Poetic Structure
#### (a) Rules:
1. **Number of Syllables:** Each line contains 6 syllables.
2. **Alliteration:** As stated in the problem.
3. **Internal Rhyme:** 
   - Let V1, V2, ..., V6 represent the vowels in each line.
   - At least one consonant immediately following V5 must immediately follow Vn (n = 1, 2, or 3).
   - In even lines, Vn = V5.

### Problem 4: Noun Suffixes
#### (a) Suffixes and Forms:
1. **Noun Suffixes:**
   - `-k2sm2` — above, overhead.
   - `-kotoya` — for.
   - `-pit` — with.

2. **Morphological Changes:**
   - After nasal consonants (m, n, N), stops p, t, k become voiced (b, d, g respectively).
   - If k comes after y, the two sounds exchange places.

3. **Possessive Pronouns:**
   - `P2s` — my
   - `mis` — your
   - If the noun begins with a stop, the consonant becomes voiced, and the corresponding nasal appears before it.

#### (a) Examples:
1. `caky2sm2tih` — right above the vine
2. `k2m2Nšeh` — like a shadow
3. `P2s mok` — my corn
4. `mis nd2ctaPm` — your teeth
5. `pahsunbit` — with the squash
6. `perolkotoyašehtaPm` — as if for the kettles

#### (b) Translations:
1. `for the chair` — `pokskukyotoya`
2. `with my kettle` — `P2s mberolpit`
3. `just like a mountain` — `koc2kšehtih`
4. `posts` — `komdaPm`
5. `above the shadows` — `k2m2Ng2sm2taPm`
6. `your town` — `mis Ngumguy`

### Problem 5: Inuktitut Sentence Structure
#### (a) Sentence Patterns:
1. **General Structure:**
   - `X-(q) V–` — `X V (himself).`
   - `X-(q) Y-(r)mik V-si–` — `X V a Y.`
   - `X-up Y-(q) V–` — `X V the Y.`

2. **Noun Endings:**
   - `-q` for definite object or subject (no definite object).
   - `-r-mik` for indefinite object.
   - To say ‘your,’ replace `-q` with `-it` and `-up` with `-vit`.

3. **Verb Suffixes:**
   - `-j` following a vowel, `-t` following a consonant.
   - Person-specific endings for subject and definite object:
     - First two patterns: `-u-tit` (2nd person), `-u-q` (3rd person).
     - Third pattern: `-a-it` (2/3), `-a-nga` (3/3), `-a-atit` (3/2).

#### (a) Translations:
1. The wolf saw your shaman.
2. Your polar bear hurt a boy.
3. Your hunter cured himself.
4. You shot the teacher.
5. You came.
6. You cured a hunter.

1. `Angatkuup aanniqtaatit.`
2. `Ilinniaqtitsijiup inuuhuktuq takujanga.` 
3. `Amaruit ukiakhaqtuq.`
4. `Qingmirmik qukiqsijutit.`
5. `Qingmiit ilinniaqtitsijimik aanniqsijuq.`
