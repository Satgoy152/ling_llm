### Problem 2: Old Norse Dróttkvætt

**(a) Rules of Dróttkvætt:**

A distich in Dróttkvætt observes these alliteration rules:

* **Line 1:** Two words within the line must alliterate.
* **Line 2:** The first word of the second line must alliterate with the alliterating sounds of the first line.
* **Vowel Alliteration:** All vowels (a, e, i, o, u, y, æ, ø, ö) and the consonant 'j' are considered to alliterate with each other.

**(b) Filling the Gaps in Stanza V:**

7. (hǫ́r vas **sǫngr** of **glymja**)
8. **Vigra** (flugbeiddra **hugfyldra**).

**Explanation:**

We choose these words based on alliteration rules and context:

* Line 1: "Svartskyggð" alliterates with "þreifsk" (both begin with 's').
* Line 2: "hlaut" alliterates with the 'h' in "Svartskyggð", and "hœgra" alliterates with "hlaut" (both begin with 'h').
* Line 3: "Rymr" alliterates with "reiddra" from line 1 (both begin with 'r').
* Line 4: "Smiði" alliterates with "seggi" (both begin with 's'). 
* Line 5: "Sigr" alliterates with "Smiði" (both begin with 's').
* Line 6: "Gauta," "ríks," and "sverð" alliterate with each other (all begin with vowels).
* Line 7: "sǫngr" alliterates with "sverð," and "glymja" alliterates with "Gauta."
* Line 8: "Vigra" alliterates with the 'v' sound in "vas" and "hugfyldra" alliterates with "hǫlða."

### Problem 3: Drehu and Cemuhî

**(a) Correspondences:**

| **Drehu**      | **English**        | **Cemuhî**   |
|-----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| drai-hmitrötr | calendar         | bé-ôdu       |
| gaa-hmitrötr  | bunch of bananas | a-pulut      |
| i-drai        | Sunday            | i-drai       |
| i-jun         | bone              | ba-bwén      |
| i-wahnawa     | church            | bé-wöli-wöta |
| jun           | awl               | tii          |
| ngöne-gejë     | fork              | ba-jié       |
| ngöne-uma      | wall              | bé-tii       |
| nyine-thin     | pencil            | bé-wöli      |
| uma-hmitrötr  | sanctuary         |  -           |

**(b) Word Meanings:**

* **wahnawa (Drehu):** Likely means "house" or "building" as it combines with "i-" to form "church" (a type of building).
* **drai (Drehu):** Probably means "day" or "sun," considering "i-drai" translates to "Sunday."
* **wöli (Cemuhî):**  Likely means "wood" or "stick," as "bé-wöli" translates to "pencil" and "bé-wöli-wöta" translates to "church" (potentially a building made of wood).
* **pulut (Cemuhî):**  Possibly means "fruit" or "cluster," as "a-pulut" translates to "bunch of bananas."

**(c) Translations from Drehu:**

* **i-bii:** bee (Following the pattern of "i-drai" for Sunday and "i-jun" for bone)
* **tusi-hmitrötr:** library (Assuming "hmitrötr" implies a collection or place related to the root word)

### Problem 5: Inuktitut

**(a) Translations into English:**

1. Amaruup angatkuit takujanga. - **The wolf saw your shaman.**
2. Nanuit inuuhukturmik aanniqsijuq. - **The polar bear hurt the boy.**
3. Angunahuktiit aarqijuq. - **Your hunter recovered/was cured.** 
4. Ilinniaqtitsiji qukiqtait. - **You shot your teacher.**
5. Qaijutit. - **You came.**
6. Angunahuktimik aarqisijutit. - **You cured the hunter.**

**(b) Translations into Inuktitut:**

1. The shaman hurt you. - **Angatkuq aanniqtuq.**
2. The teacher saw the boy. - **Ilinniaqtitsiji inuuhuktuq takujauq.**
3. Your wolf fell. - **Amaruukit ukiakhaqtuq.** 
4. You shot a dog. - **Qingmik qukiqtauq.**
5. Your dog hurt a teacher. - **Qingmiq ilinniaqtitsijimik aanniqpait.**
