### Problem 2: Old Norse Poetry

#### Part (a): Describe the rules of a dróttkvætt distich

The dróttkvætt meter is a form of Old Norse poetry characterized by:
- Each distich (pair of lines) having six syllables per line.
- Lines contain internal alliteration, where the first line of each pair has two alliterating staves, and the second line begins with a word that alliterates with them.
- All vowels alliterate with each other.
- Each line contains a specific pattern of consonants and vowel length.
