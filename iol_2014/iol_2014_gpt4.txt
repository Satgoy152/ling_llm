### Solution 4:

(a) Translate into English:

8. edèì ânò nwa. sese ozyi à? - Did this man deceive the thief?
   amemùrè wei ga ò. nwa. sese ozyi. - The girl said she did not deceive the thief.

9. amemùré le. lemu wúesè a.modhyòmù â. nò à? - Did the girl not kill this dying child?
   amemùré le. lemù wei ga òki wúesè a.modhyòmù â. nò. - This girl said that she did not kill this dying child.

10. ozyi ânò wei ga a.mó gbunono edèì. - This thief said he would heal the child.

(b) Translate into Engenni:

11. Will the old man resemble this coughing youth? - Okàá nu. amù â. nò ki.nono a.modhyòmù â. nò à?
    The child said that he[the old man] would not resemble this coughing youth. - a.mò â. nò wei ga ò. ki.nono a.modhyòmù â. nò.

12. Did this beaten woman not frighten the man? - amemùrè le. lemu wúesè edèì à?
    This beaten woman said that she[this beaten woman] did not frighten the man. - amemùrè le. lemù wei ga òki wúesè edèì.

(c) If you were to compile an Engenni dictionary, the base forms of the words "thief" and "girl" would be "ozyi" and "amemùrè" respectively. This is because these forms are used consistently across the sentences to refer to "thief" and "girl".
