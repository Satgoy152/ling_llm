Abui possessive constructions:
- Inalienable nouns (body parts) directly take possessive prefixes: 
  na- "my", a- "his/her", e- "your (sg)", ri- "our", i- "your (pl)"  
- Alienable nouns use independent possessive words:
  nefe "my", efe "your (sg)", hefe "his/her", nife "our", ife "your (pl)"
  Possessed noun is prefixed with he-.
- Recursive possession: possessed noun is treated as inalienable.

(b) Translations to Kimbundu:
16. ŋgáʃíkà? / ŋgáʃíkà nì?  
17. mútù wásáŋgá múlóʒì nì dìkámbá múkìtándà.
18. djálá djámí djámónà dìkúŋgù. 
19. mùlwándá mwálà álóʒì. / álóʒì álà mùlwándà.

Key observations:
- SVO word order
- Nouns have noun class prefixes agreeing with modifiers:
  dì- and mù- for singular, à- and mà- for plural
- Verbs agree with the subject in noun class: ŋgá- for 1sg, á- for class 1 plural, mú- for class 1 singular, etc. 
- Yes/no questions have same word order as statements
- Locative phrases use mù- "in" 
- Conjunctions: nì "and", mù "in, on"
- Genitive: N1 of N2 = N2 N1-ámì, where N1 takes the possessive suffix -ámì "my"

(c) Translations to Madak:
8. peve
9. lataamang
10. lavanga
11. lurubuno
12. luxaleng
13. luuna
14. lemparuuna

Observations:
- Plural prefixes:
  la- general plural 
  lu- dual/paucal (two, a few)
  le- plural for some nouns
- Derivational suffixes:
  -ves "heart" → "seat of emotions"
  -tli "eye" → "eye-like, round part"
  -na "tree" → "long object"
- Compounds: 
  xao "fire" + xongkao "coal"
  vat "big" + bung "collection" + menemen "village"
- Reduplication expresses diversity:
  luuna "tree" → laruuna "trees, forest"
