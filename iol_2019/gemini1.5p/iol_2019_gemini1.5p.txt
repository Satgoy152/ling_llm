## Problem 1: Yonggom

**(a)**

12. Om benu aneen.  
He takes the bread and eats it. (This sentence is identical to sentence 11)

13. Munuuna wunuub.  
We will sit down and go away.

14. Wingkiiwa wengamburuun.  
You(sg) sing and I will hear (it).

15. Anon ye weng wengamberenib yeedaraniib.  
They hear the dog and get up.

16. Oon wedmaniina karub wedmaneeb.  
I saw the fish and they saw the man.

17. Ok wedmeena aniin.  
I saw the water and drank (it).

**(b)**

18. Wenuub miniib.
19. Awon baan beneen oon ye beneen.
20. Ok ye wedmeena ambib ye wedmaan anon ye wengamberan.
21. Om banuuna wingkan.

**Explanation:**

Yonggom uses a verb-final sentence structure (Subject-Object-Verb). It seems to employ a system of suffixes to indicate tense, aspect, and possibly person/number agreement on verbs, but the data provided is not sufficient to fully analyze the verbal morphology.

## Problem 5: Nooni

**(a)**

16. bvúsɔ̀w nǔ fɔ̀ shě ntfǔː.  
Bvusɔw is the day after tomorrow.

17. me nú ŋgéː nyɛ́n kèŋkfǔ lé ɛ̀bɛ̀n.  
I will look for the yam today.

18. wvù tɔ̀ nɔ̂ː yɛ᷅n bèsèn ě bvúmbòn.  
He saw us today, on Bvumbon.

19. bèsèn nǔ bóy tɔ́ fɔ̀wǎy bvúzhı ᷇ː de᷆n.  
The woman will go to the market the day after tomorrow, on Bvuzhi.

**(b)**

The order of the Nooni days is:

1. **Bvutfu**
2. **Bvusɔw**
3. **Bvukema**
4. **Bvuŋka**
5. **Bvuŋkaden**
6. **Bvumbon**
7. **Bvuzhi**

**(c)**

20. bvúsɔ̀w nǔ me fí dìɛ̀msɛ̌n. 
21. cɔ̌ŋ cǐ nɔ̂ː cɔ́ŋ kèŋkfǔ.
22. bvûŋka᷆ːde᷆n nǔ me nú ŋgéː wengambɛ́r mutù.
23. ɛ̀bɛ̀n nǔ kwɔ᷆ːn nú géː yo᷅ dìɛ̀msɛ̌n.
24. ɛ̀bɛ̀n nǔ dìɛ̀msɛ̌n nɔ̂ː yɛ᷅n wɔ̀.

**Explanation:**

Nooni has a seven-day week cycle. Understanding the temporal adverbs and their relation to the days is key. The language also seems to have grammatical markers for future tense ("ŋgéː") and recent past ("cǐ").
