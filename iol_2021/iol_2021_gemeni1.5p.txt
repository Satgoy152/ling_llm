## Problem 1: Ekari

**(a)**

* moeto waroewo - **100** (80 + 20)
* ijema joka rati - **38**  (This is the same as "waroewoma joka rati")
* moeto wido ma benomi ka wi - **114** (80 + 20 + 10 + 4)

**(b)**

* 1 - **ka**
* 19 - **benomi ma wija ka ka** 
* 26 - **wido ma benomi** 
* 104 - **moeto daemita moeto ma wi** OR **moeto wija ma wi**
* 292 - **moeto idibi ma moeto wija ma wido ma wija**

**Explanation:**

Ekari uses a base-20 (vigesimal) numeral system.

* **Basic Numbers:** 1 (ka), 2 (wija), 3 (mepija), 4 (wi), 5 (idi), 10 (benomi), 20 (wido), 80 (moeto)
* **Combining Numbers:** Larger numbers are formed by combining these basic units additively, sometimes using "ma" as a conjunction.
* **Multiplicative Construction:** Notice the use of multiplication for some numbers (e.g., 80 is "moeto," literally "forty two").

## Problem 3: Kilivila

**(a)**

14. Navila vivila biyamata tomwaya mtona? - **How many women will look after your (sg) father?**
15. Bikamkwamsi kweyu vivila minasina. - **You (pl) will eat these fish.**
16. Amagudina gwadi lekota? - **Where did this child come from?**
17. Tevila tauwau bigisesi gugwadi gudigasisi? - **Did these men see that good woman?**
18. Legisesi ketala waga vivila minasiwena. - **Those women will see many canoes.** (OR **The women will see many canoes.**)

**(b)**

19. How many canoes did those old women see? - **Navila ka’ukwa waga legisesi yamatu?**
20. These four white men will look after this clever child. - **Biteyu tetala dimdim mtosiwena gugwadi gudikabitam.**
21. How many children will eat these pigs? - **Navila ka’ukwa gwadi biyamkwamsi kweyu bunukwa?**
22. Which woman caught those beautiful fish? - **Amtona vivila lemina kweyu minasina gudigasisi?**
23. Two wild dogs saw that old man. - **Leyawasi eyu tauwau bigisesi tomwaya.**

**Explanation:**

Kilivila grammar relies heavily on word order and particles to convey grammatical relationships.

* **Word Order:** Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) is the basic word order.
* **Question Words:** Navila (how many), Amtona (who, which), Amagudina (where)
* **Plural Markers:** -si (plural for nouns), bi- (plural for verbs)
* **Future Tense Marker:** -wena

## Problem 4: Agbirigba

**(a)**

The exception is **òtṹtṹ** (to look for). If it followed the same rules as the other Agbirigba words, its form would be **tṹòtṹtṹ**.

**(b)**

1. rʊ́tʊ̂ - chewing-stick - **tṹrʊ́tṹtʊ̂**
2. rɛẁʊ̃́ - deity - **tṹrɛẁṹtʊ̃́**
3. bіḱó - please - **túbіt́íkó**
4. ɲ́yẽ́ rɪỳã̀ - female - **tṹɲ́yẽ́tṹrɪỳã̀**
5. bɛḱʷʊ̃́ - fire - **túbɛḱṹtʷʊ̃́**
6. rіýõ̀rõ̀ - charcoal - **tіŕíýõ̀tṹrõ̀**
7. hʷɔg̀â - go out! - **tṹhʷɔg̀ṹtâ**

**Explanation:**

Agbirigba is formed by infixing **tʊ** after the first consonant of the Ogbakiri word and prefixing **t** before the first vowel. 

However, there are exceptions, as seen with "òtṹtṹ". This highlights the fact that even within a single language, different registers or styles may have their own unique rules and irregularities. 

## Problem 5: Rikbaktsa

**(a)** 

- aharapyryk - **I hugged you (pl)**
- mymyboro - **I will bite you (sg) / he will bite you (sg)** 
- uru - **he slept**
- tsiksiwynaha - **we left**
- ikwak - **I hunted / he hunted**
- ziksi - **he left**

**(b)** 

- we will fall - **iknaranaraha**
- I bathed - **imorona**
- you (sg) bit us - **tsiboroha**
- I will wait for them / he will wait for them - **apery**
- I will hug you (pl) / he will hug you (pl) - **aharapyryk**
- you (sg) will leave - **tsiksi**
- I killed it / he killed it - **ibeze** 

**Explanation:**

Rikbaktsa verbs are highly inflected, with prefixes and suffixes marking subject, object, and tense.

* **Subject Prefixes:** a- (1sg), tsi- (2sg), ni- (3sg), pi- (1/3sg future), i- (1/3sg past)
* **Object Suffixes:** -ryk (2pl), -ha (1pl), -na (3pl)
* **Plural:** -wyna- (pluralizer for verbs)
* **Future Tense:** -ru (future tense marker)

By analyzing the provided examples and understanding the function of these prefixes and suffixes, we can translate between English and Rikbaktsa.
