**Assignment 2. The marks ` and ´ indicate the stress in certain Catalan words. Find out when they are used.**

* **` (grave accent):** Used when the stress falls on the penultimate syllable and the word ends in a vowel, -s, or -n. 
* **´ (acute accent):** Used when the stress falls on the last syllable or on the penultimate syllable and the word ends in a consonant other than -s or -n.

**Assignment 3. Which syllable is stressed in those words where there is no ` or ´ mark?**

In words without accent marks, the stress falls on the **penultimate syllable**. This is the default stress pattern in Catalan.

**Assignment 3. Do you think the following phrases are possible in Udihe?**

1. bi xabai: **Not possible.**  Body parts require possessive suffixes.
2. su b’ataŋiu bugdiŋini: **Possible.**  Means "your son's leg".
3. si igi: **Not possible.** Body parts require possessive suffixes.

**Assignment 4. Translate the following phrases into English and explain how their meanings differ:**

1. bi tekpui: my skin  (refers to skin detached from the body)
2. bi tekpuŋi:: my head's skin (refers to skin still attached to the head)

The difference lies in the use of the possessive suffix -ŋi:.  It indicates that the skin is still part of the head.

### Problem 5 (Ngoni)

**Assignment. Each of the Ngoni sentences (11a-c) and (12a-c) contains an error. Translate these sentences into English, explain what the error is in each case, and then correct it, giving for each example four correct sentences in Ngoni that describe the same situation.**

**11. (a) Mdala guli ugimbi Mwangi.**

* **Translation:** The woman bought Mwangi beer.
* **Error:** The verb "guli" agrees with "mdala" (singular), but the object "ugimbi" requires a plural verb form. 
* **Four correct sentences:**
    * Mdala aguli Mwangi ugimbi. 
    * Mdala amgulira Mwangi ugimbi.
    * Mdala wamgulira Mwangi ugimbi. 
    * Mdala amgulire Mwangi ugimbi. 

**11. (b) Mdala mguli Mwangi ugimbi.**

* **Translation:** The woman she-bought Mwangi beer.
* **Error:** Redundant subject marker "m" before the verb. 
* **Four correct sentences:**
    * Mdala guli Mwangi ugimbi. 
    * Mdala aguli Mwangi ugimbi.
    * Mdala amgulira Mwangi ugimbi.
    * Mdala wamgulira Mwangi ugimbi.

**11. (c) Mdala aguli ugimbi Mwangi.**

* **Translation:** The woman he-bought beer Mwangi.
* **Error:** Incorrect subject marker "a" before the verb. It should agree with "mdala" (female).
* **Four correct sentences:**
    * Mdala guli Mwangi ugimbi. 
    * Mdala aguli Mwangi ugimbi. 
    * Mdala amgulira Mwangi ugimbi. 
    * Mdala wamgulira Mwangi ugimbi. 

**12. (a) Kamau vamguli vabuya mene.**

* **Translation:** Kamau they-bought the grandmothers a goat.
* **Error:** Incorrect subject marker "va" before the verb. It should agree with "Kamau" (singular).
* **Four correct sentences:**
    * Kamau avaguli vabuya mene. 
    * Kamau avagulira vabuya mene.
    * Kamau wabagulira vabuya mene. 
    * Kamau awagulire vabuya mene.

**12. (b) Kamau guli mene vabuya.**

* **Translation:** Kamau bought a goat the grandmothers. 
* **Error:**  The verb "guli" agrees with "Kamau" (singular), but the object "vabuya" requires a plural verb form.
* **Four correct sentences:**
    * Kamau avaguli vabuya mene. 
    * Kamau avagulira vabuya mene.
    * Kamau wabagulira vabuya mene. 
    * Kamau awagulire vabuya mene.

**12. (c) Kamau vaguli vabuya mene.**

* **Translation:**  Kamau they-bought the grandmothers a goat.
* **Error:** Incorrect subject marker "va" before the verb. It should agree with "Kamau" (singular).
* **Four correct sentences:**
    * Kamau avaguli vabuya mene. 
    * Kamau avagulira vabuya mene.
    * Kamau wabagulira vabuya mene.
