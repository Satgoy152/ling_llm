### Problem 1: Verb Morphology and Syntax
#### Rules:
1. **Word Order:** Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).
2. **Article:** `ki` (used after nouns).
3. **Interrogative Marker:** Sentences end in `he` to indicate a question.
4. **Subject and Object Markers on Verbs:**

| Subject | Object |
|---------|--------|
| 1sg     | wa-    |
| 2sg     | ya-    |
| 3sg     | 0      |
| 3pl     | -pi    |
| -       | wičha- |

5. **Order of Prefixes:** Object prefix followed by subject prefix.
6. **Number of Nouns:** Expressed only by the verbs.
7. **Coordinating Conjunctions:**
   - `ną` — same subject.
   - `čha` — different subjects.

#### Translations:
a. **Assignment 1:**
   1. I came and the Indians called the bears.
   2. The man killed you and called me.
   3. The man killed you and he called me.
   4. He killed you.

b. **Assignment 2:**
   1. Whom did he kill?
   2. Who killed him?

3. **Assignment 3:**
   1. `lakhota ki hokšita ki ktepi čha matho ki hi`
   2. `yahi ną lakhota ki yakte`
   3. `tuwa wačho he`
   4. `wičhaša ki hipi čha tuwa wičhakte`

### Problem 2: Stress and Article Usage
#### Observations:
1. **Articles:**
   - `el` (masculine), `els` (plural masculine)
   - `la` (feminine), `les` (plural feminine)
   
2. **Stress Marking:**
   - `\`` on `e`, `o`, `a`.
   - `´` on `i`, `u`.
   - If a word ends in `-s` and the stress is not penultimate, it is marked explicitly.

3. **Plural Forms:**
   - Masculine nouns: `-os` if stressed on the last syllable, `-s` otherwise.
   - Feminine nouns: Only `-s`.

#### Examples:
1. **Assignment 1:**
   | Singular           | Plural         |
   |--------------------|----------------|
   | el bastaix         | els bastaixos  |
   | el troleibús       | els troleibusos|
   | la clos            | les clos       |
   | el iris            | els iris       |
   | el llaç            | els llaços     |
   | el ònix            | els ònixs      |
   | la pelvis          | les pelvis     |
   | el pis             | els pisos      |
   | el sequaç          | els sequaços   |
   | el sequaç          | les sequaçs    |
   | la xeix            | les xeixs      |

2. **Assignment 2:** The marks `\`` and `´` are used when the stress falls on a non-default syllable.

3. **Assignment 3:** Without the marks, stress is penultimate if the word ends in `-s`, otherwise it is final.

### Problem 4: Possession and Pronouns
#### Pronouns and Possession:

Assignment a.
su b’ataŋiu zä:ŋini - your son’s money
si teŋku bugdiŋi: - thy stool leg
si teŋkuŋi: bugdini - thy stool’s leg

Assignment b.
the boy’s thigh - b’ata wo:ni
our boar - bu naktaŋiu
my daughter’s tree - bi azigaŋi mo:ŋini

Assignment c.
bi xabai - Yes: ‘my udder’ (a cow speaking, shall we say, in a fairy-tale)
su b’ataŋiu bugdiŋini - Yes: ‘your son’s leg’ (not his own, though, but perhaps a chicken’s leg
belonging to him)
si igi - No (‘my tail’ would be si igi:)

Assignment d.
bi tekpui ‘my skin’ (the skin of my body)
bi tekpuŋi: ‘my skin’ (e.g., an animal’s skin belonging to me)
1. **Possessive Pronouns:**
   | Person | Singular | Plural  |
   |--------|----------|---------|
   | 1st    | bi       | bu      |
   | 2nd    | si       | su      |

2. **Possessive Markers:**
   - `-i, -ni, -u` indicate possessor’s person and number.
   - `-ŋi` indicates alienable possession.

3. **Examples:**
   | Expression            | Translation       |
   |-----------------------|-------------------|
   | bi wo:i               | my cow            |
   | si bogdoloi           | your son          |
   | bi mo:ŋi:             | my tree           |
   | si ja:ŋi:             | your field        |
   | ja: xabani            | his udder         |
   | bu naktaŋiu           | our boar          |

### Problem 5: Verb Structure and Sentence Order
#### Verb Construction:
1. **Prefixes:**
   - First prefix denotes the number of the subject.
   - Second prefix denotes the number of the indirect object.
   - The number of the direct object is not marked.

2. **Word Orders:**
   - (1) S V DO IO
   - (2) S V IO DO

3. **Rules:**
   - Both S and IO must be marked if word order is (1).
   - Marking is optional in order (2) but follows specific rules.

Assignment:
Clearly sentences (11) mean ‘The woman bought the beer for Mwangi’. This can be said
correctly in the following four ways:
Mdala amguli ugimbi Mwangi
Mdala amguli Mwangi ugimbi
Mdala aguli Mwangi ugimbi
Mdala guli Mwangi ugimbi
The errors are:
(11a) word order (1), but neither S nor IO marked in the verb.
(11b) only IO marked, breaking (*).
(11c) word order (1), but only S marked.

Sentences (12) mean ‘Kamau bought the goat for the grandmothers’. That is:
Kamau avaguli mene vabuya
Kamau avaguli vabuya mene
Kamau aguli vabuya mene
Kamau guli vabuya mene
The errors are:
(12a) the numbers of both S and IO incorrectly marked.
(12b) word order (1), but neither S nor IO marked in the verb.
(12c) only IO marked, breaking (*). (Or else: IO not marked, which word order (2) allows,
but number of S marked incorrectly).
