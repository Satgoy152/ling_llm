### Problem 1: Numeral Construction in Sulka Language
#### Rules:
1. **Numerals:**
   - `tgiang` — 1
   - `lomin` — 2
   - `korlotge` — 3
   - `korlolo` — 4
   - `ktiëk` — 5
   - `mhelom` — 20

2. **Modifiers:**
   - `hori orom` — addition
   - `lo` — doubling

3. **Number Agreement:**
   - `a` — singular
   - `o` — plural (from 3 on)

4. **Special Forms:**
   - `ngaitegaap` — foursome of breadfruits
   - `moulang` — twosome of breadfruits
   - `ngaitegaap` — foursome of coconuts

#### (a) Translations:
1. `a ksie a tgiang` — 1 coconut
2. `o ngaitegaap a korlotge` — 12 breadfruits
3. `o ngausmia a ktiëk` — 20 coconuts
4. `o vuo a lo ktiëk hori orom a tgiang` — 11 betel nuts

#### (b) Translations:
1. 2 yams — `a lo tu a lomin`
2. 14 yams — `o sngu a lo ktiëk hori orom a korlolo`
3. 15 breadfruits — `o ngaitegaap a korlotge hori orom a moulang hori orom a tgiang`
4. 20 betel nuts — `o vuo a mhelom`

### Problem 4: Stress Patterns in Sanskrit
#### Rules:
1. If the stop consonant in the root and the vowel in the suffix are voiced, the stress is on the suffix.
2. If the stop consonant in the root and the vowel in the suffix are voiceless, the stress is on the root.

#### (a) Additional Rule:
- This rule applies if the root contains precisely one stop consonant. If there are two (e.g., `bhāg-a-`, `pad-a-`, `pat-i-`) or none (`us-ri-`), the stress cannot be determined.

#### (b) Examples:
1. `mr.dh-rá-`
2. `phé-na-`
3. `stu-tí-`
4. `tan-tí-`
5. `bhār-á-`
6. `dū-tá-`
7. `sváp-na-`
8. `bh´̄u-mi-`
9. `ghar-má-`
10. `abh-rá-`
11. `ghan-á-`
12. `ghŕ.s.-vi-`

### Problem 5: Nahuatl Verb Conjugation
#### Rules:
1. **Verb Prefixes:**
   - Subject: `ni-` (1st person sg), `ti-` (2nd person sg), `——` (3rd person sg)
   - Object: `nēch-` (1st person sg), `mitz-` (2nd person sg), `k-` (3rd person sg)
   - Additional Object: `tē-` (somebody), `tla-` (something)

2. **Verb Suffixes:**
   - `make . . .`:
     - `<intransitive verb>-tia` (with lengthening of preceding `i`)
     - `<transitive verb>-ltia`
   - `do for . . .`: `-lia` (with change of preceding `a` to `i`)

#### (a) Translations:
1. `tiktlazohtlaltia` — you make the woman love the carpenter.
   - `in zihuātl in kuauhx̄ınki` — you make the carpenter love the woman.
2. `nēchtzāhtz̄ıtia` — he makes me shout.
3. `tikhūıteki` — you beat him.
4. `nikēhuilia in kikatl in t̄ız̄ıtl` — I sing the song for the healer.
5. `nikneki in ātōlli` — I want the atole.
6. `mitztlakāhualtia` — he makes you leave something.

#### (b) Translations:
1. He makes me prepare the atole: `nēchch̄ıhualtia in ātōlli`
2. You prepare the wine for somebody: `tiktēch̄ıhuilia in oktli`
3. The healer makes you sleep: `mitzkoch̄ıtia in t̄ız̄ıtl`
4. I sing something: `nitlaēhua`
5. I fall: `nihuetzi`
