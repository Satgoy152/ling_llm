## Problem 2 Solution:

**(a) Hieroglyphic Matching:**

1. runtiyas ‘deer’ ∼ **D. 5,  N. 5Ts** 
2. patis ‘foot’  ∼ **B. +, V. ´ys**
3. harnisas ‘fortress’ ∼ **J. H!,  X. H´%**
4. iziyanta ‘they made’ ∼  **F. A50´,  T. A50t**
5. turpis ‘bread’ ∼ **G. å, P. å0T**
6. tarhunzas ‘thunder’ ∼ **H. „0s,  Z. A°s** 
7. hawis ‘sheep’ ∼ **C. HWs,  K. qHWs**
8. sanawas ‘good’ ∼  **I. sNWs,  W. vås**
9. nimuwizas ‘son’ ∼ **L. v,  M. f4Wis**
10. zitis ‘man’ ∼ **S. y2is,  tyis** (from part b) 
11. piyanti ‘they give’ ∼ **Q. ÚWs,  U. T** 
12. hantawatis ‘king’ ∼ **A. HtWTs,  O. !fss**
13. istaris ‘hand’ ∼ **E. Ts,  R. °**

**(b) Additional Word Identification:**

1. **tyis** represents **zitis ‘man’** 
2. **f4_** represents **nimuwizas ‘son’**

**(c)  Luwian Symbol Functions:**

1. **´:**  Indicates a plural form (e.g., hands, feet)
2. **%:** Represents a fortified place or structure, likely a phonetic component related to "harnisas"
3. **W:**  A phonetic component representing the sound "w"
4. **t:**  A phonetic component representing the sound "t"
5. **s:** A common grammatical marker, potentially for plurals or possessive forms
6. **T:** Likely represents a deity or a concept related to divinity
7. **5:**  Appears to be a logogram or ideogram representing a horned animal, possibly a deer.
